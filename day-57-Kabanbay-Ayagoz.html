Stuttgart - Vladivostok 2018

Day 57, Kabanbay (Kazakhstan) - Ayagoz (Kazakhstan)

Date Sa 01 September 2018 By Helmut Dittrich, Translation by Julie Herberger-Dittrich Category english Tags 09. Week / Kazakhstan / Kabanbay / Ayagoz / flat tire

The host is very grumpy and dismissive. Finally the employees make eggs and tea for breakfast for us. Wifi is tough and I do not get an upload.

There are many Landcruisers, a dried up lake with a wrinkled road. Charlie discovers a broken spoke behind and shortly thereafter he has a flat in front. He reaches us via Whatsapp 25km further as we arrive at the truckstop. We drive back and meet him with a Land Cruiser with young people from Almaty who are on their way back from Russia.

Charlie has already removed the broken inner tube. Andre and Charlie are installing a new one. Meanwhile, a mo-ped driver comes by and asks if he can have two liters of fuel. I tap in 3 liters and he is happy. He is Australian and has started his trip in London and also belongs to the Mongol Rally and wants to go to Russia. He is in a hurry because his visa expires soon.

We continue to the truck-stop, where we had been before the breakdown and eat a snack there. Again, the Google translator is used and Andre and his picture dictionary. After some time we see that the weather does not change, so we move on. The road annoys us because it leads to faster driving, which is often acknowledged by the vibration of the suspension.

After a refueling stop we reach Ayagoz and a short time later and try to find a place to stay. After the 3rd try, it works and we get into a brand new hotel on the main street, which offers western standards at moderate prices.

We call a taxi, which is immediately there but can not drive, since there is no more fuel in the tank. So, again, I tap off 2 liters of mine. When the taxi is filled it goes first to the gas station. First we must wait because the place has no electricity or fuel or both. After 10 minutes something goes right and we drive into the city to eat at a restaurant.

Again, thank our Google translator we enjoy communicating and it is fun for all parties. The menu is out of action today, but there is shashlik duck, chicken or lamb with fries and beer. What more is needed?

We call our well-known taxi driver again. The fuel seems to be still in it, but it does not start despite light blows on the starter..... Only by the passengers getting out to push it.....does it work! The driver finds this very funny. The weather is fresh and my cold gets worse.
