Stuttgart - Vladivostok 2018

Day 23, Shiraz (Iran)

Date So 29 Juli 2018 By Helmut Dittrich, Translation by Julie Herberger-Dittrich Category english Tags 4. Week / Iran / Shiraz

After a quick breakfast, we sit the taxi out of the heat and into the comfort of the air-condition. We travel to our desired destination and it's easy to sit in the air-conditioned taxi and swim in the stream of traffic. It is nice to have time to watch the action on the streets.

We start with the Nasir al-Molk Mosque also called the Pink Mosque. The colored window glass floods the prayer room into a multitude of colors when the sun enters.

Worth seeing are also the Muqarnas, a 'stalactite vault' used in Islamic architecture for the transition between a square base and a dome.


Nasir al-Molk Mosque

On religious questions, a very friendly minister answers questions about Islam.


Nasir al-Molk Mosque

Then we drive to the citadel of Karim Khan, who during his rule, declared Shiraz as the capital.

The attached workshop of artisans impressively shows us which work steps and craftsmanship are necessary to make artful jewelry boxes with miniatures.


Handicrafts in Karim Khan citadel


Handicrafts in Karim Khan citadel

The botanical garden Eram also called Paradise Garden unfortunately reveals itself not to its best splendor, as the season is now used to carry out renovation work and all of the streams are dry. But, it gives an idea of ​​the splendor which it once had at the height of its flowering.


Botanical Garden Eram


Botanical Garden Eram


Botanical Garden Eram

We drive back to the hotel as the heat sets in and enjoy listening to the piano playing in the lobby while we drink tea and work on our records. As we learn, the musician studied piano and other instruments and has a wide repertoire which he presents to our delight.

As the heat wears off and the light gets better for taking pictures, we take a taxi to the tomb of Hafez the famous Persian poet.

The mausoleum is very beautiful and very busy, as it seems to be a popular meeting place. Also, a religious event is being held with songs. The place is very relaxing and many have gotten dressed up and there are a lot of photos being taken.

We received an offer to go tomorrow, about 60km by taxi, to Persepolis, which is the capital of the ancient Persian Empire. There will also be a guide who will explain the ancient site to us. We quickly make the price and the appointment via WhatsApp clear and are looking forward to tomorrow.
