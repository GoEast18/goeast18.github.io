Stuttgart - Vladivostok 2018

Day 31, Mary (Turkmenistan) - Bukhara (Boxoro) (Uzbekistan)

Date Mo 06 August 2018 By Helmut Dittrich, Translation by Julie Herberger-Dittrich Category english Tags 5. Week / Mary / Turkmenistan / Desert / Desert Karakum / Bukhara / Boxoro / Uzbekistan / silk road

When packing the motorcycles in front of Hotel Mary we meet a Polish Antonov pilot who just came from India and have a little chat with him. He speaks German well and says he will fly on to Kuwait.


Hotel Mary


Turkmenian fashion


Hotel Mary

We're leaving and getting out of Mary is going very well. The roads are wide and little traffic. We see very clean-plastered buildings and monuments commissioned by the Potentate. The streets in the city are far too wide, but for us it's better to have less traffic than traffic jams.

After the city limits, the road is very brittle and littered with countless potholes.

It is incomprehensible how such a rich country can have such stone-age roads. And this on the main traffic artery.

Driving in the heat it settles down to mid-40 degrees celcius in addition there still comes a hot desert wind, which blows us properly. Driving with an open visor is unthinkable. Many police checks, but leave us alone. The uniforms remind me of VoPos.

Again we see dromedaries on the roadside and take water at a rest stop.

Türkmenabad just before the border

The last 50km from the 250km to the border town bother me and I should have taken another break. Arrived at the border, I'm feeling pretty flat.

We meet the Swiss motorcyclists from Zurich, whose machines we had seen at the hotel in Mary. We refuel again and the departure procedure begins.

Giving GPS, unloading luggage, checking here, getting the same questions there. Always make friendly face, we are ambassadors of our country, keep smiling and my stomach rebels and I'm very slow. Only a honey lolly-pop and a liter of water pump me up again and then the entire process goes on to the Uzbek side. The Swiss are already through and we arrange for the hotel Malika in Bukhara.

Here it goes off quickly and the method has a logical consequence. After a total of 3 hours we have the border clearance behind us and it continues the last 100km to Bukhara. The road is better here in Uzbekistan.


Cotton fields in Uzbekistan

We reach Bukhara, which is also on the silk road. It is well visited with international guests and we feel like we are back on planet earth.

Wifi, WhatsApp, ATM, no black market, everything was relaxed. Laughing faces. What a depressing country Turkmenistan was. We meet the Swiss motorcyclist Raimund and Philipp again and spend the evening in the restaurant with good food and beer and exchange travel experiences.

The Turkmen national currency, of which we still have a few bills, no one wants to have here, let alone swap. I give the gentleman at the hotel reception a note which he attaches to his album. Turkmenistan I have hacked off for ever.
