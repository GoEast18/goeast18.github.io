Stuttgart - Vladivostok 2018

Day 74, Ulan Bator (Mongolia)

Date Di 18 September 2018 By Helmut Dittrich, Translation by Julie Herberger-Dittrich Category english Tags 11. Week / Mongolia / Ulan-Bator

As soon as I pick up my reserved UAZ off-road vehicle and Charlie, Andre and Martin get their KTM motorcycles, we can begin. The city is pretty clogged and so we make a meeting place outside of town.
Driving with the UAZ is very adventurous. No power steering and no brake booster lend to pure driving pleasure. The engine is very elastic and with 30km / h in 3rd gear, it has great acceleration. Maximum speed is 80km / h. It handles easy on slopes and does not force you to downshift.

The noise level in the interior is acceptable and allows for easy conversation. I'm used to other things from my Land Rover 109er experience. The technology of the UAZ is stone age but obviously mature and reliable.

The driver or owner is sitting next to me and he reassures me after a short while that I am a good driver. The conversation with him is done almost exclusively with Google translator because his English is not very proficient.

Under the beautiful blue sky and sunshine we have moderate temperatures and great scenery. We visit the Genghis Khan equestrian statue, which is located about 50km southeast of Ulan Bator. It reaches 30m high up with 250 tones of stainless steel keeping it steady. After this great impression, we drive to the Gorkhi Terelj National Park and look at the Turtle Rock formation that truly looks like a turtle.


turtle rock

I exchange my driver seat for the passenger seat to enjoy the scenery and sun and to take some more photos. The driver shows me how you can drive the UAZ differently. While I was practicing cautious driving, the other driver puts the pedal to the metal and shows me which holes the UAZ can handle and how to turn it. He has a very respectable driving tactic. The car would certainly be an asset to people who often travel in nature and choose routes that are not suitable for everyday cars.

The guys with the Enduros are still cruising in the area and we make an appointment to meet up again at the Oasis. Driving through the city traffic jams feels like torture in the UAZ, but we eventually make it through the city to the Oasis. I say goodbye to the UAZ and driver and will certainly not forget the memorable driving pleasure I had. Today we received the confirmation for the train tickets and on Friday we will start in the direction of Moscow.
