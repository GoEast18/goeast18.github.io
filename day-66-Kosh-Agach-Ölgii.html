Go East

Stuttgart - Vladivostok 2018

Day 66, Kosh-Agach (Russia) - Ölgii (Mongolia)

Date Mo 10 September 2018 By Helmut Dittrich, Translation by Julie Herberger-Dittrich Category english Tags 10. Week / Russia / West-Siberia / Altai / Kosh-Agach / Ölgii / Mongolia

We leave at 6 degrees and reach the border at 2,000m altitude in Tashanta. It was snowing last night and here we are now at the snow line. The generator of the tank is broken and so it looks bad with fuel. When we arrive at the border, we first have to wait. There is block clearance. When the block being processed is ready, lunch is for an hour. It is still under 10 degrees and now the sun comes out a bit and there is no wind. We have a picnic with Russian salami, mustard, bread and coffee and tea, which we had made this morning and wait. Slowly, the cold also crawls through the soles of the shoes and the waiting becomes tough.

Finally it goes on and now it is unloading luggage and drag to the scanner. Everything is completely correct and finally we are done and it goes on. The next 20km are still fine. Last Russian post. He opens the gate and releases us on the now following runway for 5km until we meet the Mongolian post, which is mostly run by uniformed women. Short wait here. We pass through various stations without baggage searches and are now in Mongolia.

A vehicle insurance must be completed. We can also exchange our remaining Kazakh and Russian money there and finally move on. We expect 100km of slopes. After 20km, the track is over and it continues on great asphalt. The road leads to an altitude of 2,000m to 2,600m with some great scenery and still under 10 degrees with sunshine.


Insurance office and currency exchange on the Mongolian border

We arrive at Ölgii and find Hotel Duman, quite finished and demolished for 3 stars but better than unheated yurts like Martin and Andre had. From Martin and Andre we learn at dinner in the restaurant via WhatsApp that they had a difficult leg to the north today. 200km of piste with sand fields, gravel and mud holes. You needed 8 hours. We are now considering taking the southern route, as it should be a bit warmer south and the road should be busier. The cold and the rain probably come earlier this year. We are just 2-3 weeks late for the middle route, as Martin and Andre drive now.
