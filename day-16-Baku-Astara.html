Stuttgart - Vladivostok 2018

Day 16, Baku (Azerbaijan) - Astara (Azerbaijan)

Date So 22 Juli 2018 By Helmut Dittrich, Translation by Julie Herberger-Dittrich Category english Tags Azerbaijan / Baku / Astara / 03. Week / Oil refill / handlebar plate / Caspian Sea


Astara


Empty road in the morning at 7:30


Empty road in the morning at 7:30

It's already 28 degrees celsius in the morning at 7:30. Our breakfast restaurant, which is open 24 hours and located next to our hotel, is empty. The city is still sleeping. I fill after 5,000 km mileage 0.5 L. oil on the motorcycle. At 9:30 we drive south on a highway whose section is so new that it is not yet in the navigation map.

The landscape changes between steppe and farmland. The section of the new motorway ends and we continue on the old route. This is a much repaired road with sinks that has suffered from heavy traffic.

We pass villages with bustling life. Melons, tomatoes etc. are offered everywhere on the roadside. We stop at a rest stop in a grove consisting of small huts and get excellent kebabs. The young man who brings the food tells us that he is studying to be physics teacher in Baku.


resting place


resting place


resting place

Today's sultriness with 35+ degrees celsius and the food sets us so today that we have to take a short break before we arrive in Astara. Astara is the border town to Iran and is separated by the border river Astarachay. We find the hotel Sindan and are the only guests and get the room for the equivalent of 15,- Eur.


Hotel Sindan

The handlebar plate on the motorcycle has loosened a bit through the many potholes and makes noise while driving. This is quickly fixed with the 36 ring spanner we have for such cases. The screw is tightened and also secured with RapidFuse adhesive.

In the adjoining restaurant we get a simple meal and the last beer for the next days, because in Iran there is a ban on alcohol. All the people are very friendly and want to know where we come from and what the machines cost. Many also want a selfie with us.
