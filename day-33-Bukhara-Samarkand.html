Stuttgart - Vladivostok 2018

Day 33, Bukhara (Boxoro) (Uzbekistan) - Samarkand (Uzbekistan)

Date Mi 08 August 2018 By Helmut Dittrich, Translation by Julie Herberger-Dittrich Category english Tags 5. Week / Bukhara / Boxoro / Uzbekistan / silk road / Samarkand

I fix the sheepskin on my seat and Charlie's sheepskin supplier has also already delivered, then we start.

to Samarkand

From pothole to super highway everything is there. The temperatures are rising, although we are gaining altitude. On the road we see mainly Chevrolets in white, very small minibuses that are used as shared taxis and of course Lada. Trucks of course you see the Russian Kamaz, then MAN, Daimler and DAF and sporadic Volvo. All German bodybuilders are represented, as many trailers and trailers drove in Germany and often still carry the German company names.

While we drive so thoughtfully with our cruising speed of 95-100km / h before us (more is not even possible because of the potholes), there suddenly jumps out of nowhere a 'highwayman' traffic policeman onto the road and causes us to stop.

Immediately our passport, driver's license etc are cashed in and his colleague shows us based on their radar gun that we should have driven 91km / h. Since 70km / h should be allowed, we have been too fast. We had not seen a sign showing only 50s and 30s.

In short, the two make it clear that there is a protocol and it should cost 230, - USD together. With age discount and tourist discount we come to 150, - and within minutes to 100, until the negotiation room was exhausted. The one policeman is still interested in Charlie's sheepskin, but get dumped.

We start again and try to keep the limit of 70km / h. In fact, we see more police with the speed pistol, but now we get off scot-free.

After some time we stop at a truck stop and I inspect the kitchen to see what's being offered. The ladies do not pose for photos and I decide for Uzbek Maultaschen Manti with salad, which taste excellent. When we start driving, Charlie realizes his sheepskin is missing. One of the policemen must have clamped down on that when we were still negotiating. Annoying.

We drive on and make a detour to side roads, which we then cancel, because the road condition would push our cruising speed to 40km/h on average.


to Samarkand

We arrive at the Hotel Orient Star in Samarkand and meet again the couple that participates in the Mongolia rally that we met at the border. The winning team will be the last to arrive in a time slot. Only older vehicles are allowed and maximum 1200ccm.


Rally car

We hang out at the pool, the last pool we had in Ganja (Azerbaijan) and enjoy it. We unfortunately do not meet the Swiss anymore, as they obviously already have another date. We take a taxi to the restaurant Platan and enjoy excellent food, beer and wine with discreet music and ambience.
