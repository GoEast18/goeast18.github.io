Stuttgart - Vladivostok 2018

Day 19, Tehran (Iran)

Date Mi 25 Juli 2018 By Helmut Dittrich, Translation by Julie Herberger-Dittrich Category english Tags 3. Week / Iran / Tehran

I check the internet and notice that different sites are blocked like mirrors online, Facebook, PayPal, DataQuest etc.

The breakfast buffet is great and rich and you can order the eggs in all variants. There is a special type of bread (Sangak) baked in a stone oven, which is like little pebbles. When removing are then sometimes stones on the bread, which must then be stripped. It is just a delicious bread!


breakfast


breakfast

I'm contacting Shahla, a friend from Julie, and we're meeting tonight. We take a taxi to the Golestan Palast and enjoy the ride to the full, not having to be tortured by the traffic madness.

At the palace there is background music, Nights in white satin 'with oriental instruments. Not bad. The exhibits are very interesting and fortunately most of the rooms have air conditioning. At 38 degrees this is very helpful. We meet a high school class and they have an english project. Meaning, they interview the tourists they meet. They are bothering us and we are talking about our journey so far and our motorcycles and that we all really like the people in Iran, because they have all been very friendly to us so far. In the end we are the heroes and there are 'a thousand' selfies with us.

The bazaar of Tehran){:target="_blank"}, which is considered one of the largest covered bazaars in the world, is not far from the palace and so we go on foot, standing in every bit of shade there. There are armies of porters pulling small carriages and transporting goods to the bazaar.

The bazaar offers everything from zip fastener parts to carpets, shoes and, of course, tons of gold jewelery sold by weight and up to the minute gold prices.

We are looking for a well-known and good restaurant and can finally only find it with the help of a pilot in the winding streets and alleys. A long line of people are waiting for admission. When we finally get there, a staff member helps us with the selection from the menu, because unfortunately only written in Farsi. We take a seat and shortly thereafter are served. Extremely delicious and now we realize why the crowd is so big.


Cellar restaurant with rush


Cellar restaurant with rush

After the meal is over, the 'soup coma' comes and we take a taxi, another experience that will not make you fall asleep, to the Hotel Ferdowsi, where we thankfully let the air conditioning cool us down.

At 5:30 come Shahla and her husband Mohammed. We drink tea, learn a lot about life here and finally make a tour around the 'Block', where we learn some information about the museums and buildings and try one or the other delicacies from the street booths. Back at the hotel, we finish our travel route planning for Iran.
