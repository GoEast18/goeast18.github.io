Stuttgart - Vladivostok 2018

Day 37, Dushanbe (Tajikistan)

Date So 12 August 2018 By Helmut Dittrich, Translation by Julie Herberger-Dittrich Category english Tags 06. Week / silk road / Dushanbe / Tajikistan / Mongol Rally

The night was bearable as it cooled off well. The bath and toilet situation is a bit tense, since the 65 people of the rally have to be distributed between 2 toilets. Accordingly, long waiting times are inevitable, after which we rebook into a vacant room with air conditioning and a bathroom.

Michael and Heike from Rhineland-Pfalz, Germany arrive and we exchange information. They flew in with their bicycles from Frankfurt and want to explore the Pamir with the bicycles.

The weather has not improved and one could think that it is like sitting in a huge dustbell.


City Hostel

From the manager of the hostel we learn the current road conditions of the planned route. We also hear that the terrorists, who a few days ago drove by car into a group of four bicycle tourists and stabbed them, were killed by the police. It was said to have been a group of individuals that had radicalized over the Internet. So the track should be safe and passable again.

The route along the Afghan border should also be safe, fuel supply also secured and accommodation options should also exist.

The rally participants depart. Let's see if we'll meet them again, because the track will be similar.

Charlie and I dream of participating in such a rally before we reach 70, but then air conditioning should be in the car.

At 12:00 the temperature has reached 36 degrees. Time to retire to cooled rooms. After the obligatory nap I write and we have nice conversations with Heike and Michael, who put their bikes together.

In the evening, a smaller group of the Mongol Rally arrives, including a German from Stuttgart. With Harry, a filmmaker from the UK, who also rides in the rally. We have a nice conversation. He wants to cut the documentation footage after the trip.

We take a taxi with Michael and Heike and drive to a restaurant near the Botanical Gardens. It consists of several pavilions, which are equipped as dining spaces seperated by curtains. We eat together and drink beer and spend a nice evening. I then show the taxi driver the way back to the City Hostel on the basis of the previously downloaded offline map from Google maps. What would I do without a smartphone with all the technology in all the Russian speechlessness we have?
