Stuttgart - Vladivostok 2018

Day 35, Samarkand (Usbekistan)

Date Fr 10 August 2018 By Helmut Dittrich, Translation by Julie Herberger-Dittrich Category english Tags 5. Week / Uzbekistan / silk road / Samarkand / fuel tank repair

The tank is being rebuilt and we are clearing out of the room at the Hotel Orient, as the rooms have already been reserved.


Fuel tank repair


Fuel tank repair

We have our luggage transported by taxi to the next hotel and drive with the motorcycles afterwards. The hotel is located next to the Bibi-Chanum mosque we visited yesterday and is called Bibikhanum Hotel with a beautiful courtyard and pleasant rooms.


Bibikhanum Hotel


Bibikhanum Hotel


Bibikhanum Hotel


Bibikhanum Hotel

With horror, I realize that my phone is missing. I drive back to the hotel Orient and there it is already ready to pick up at the reception. The absence has already terrified me and suddenly you become aware of how dependent you are on the stupid thing and what kind of loss it would cause.

After a nap during the heat of the day, we take the electric taxi in the pedestrian zone in front of the mosque and drive up to the Registan. It has now cooled to 35 degrees and the evening light sets in. Around 7:30 pm the sun goes down. I sat opposite the Registan in the park with plane trees populated by thousands of birds with loud twittering and watch the goings on.

The Registan now appears in the evening light in pastel shades. Wedding parties use the light for the photo shoot with an impressive backdrop. People, young and old sit at tables of park drinking coffee with lemonade or ice cream and enjoy the evening and take countless photos.

The youth is predominantly dressed western, headscarves are rather the exception. They show legs and arms here in this area. It is striking also to see the great wealth of children. The shared taxi service no longer drives in the dark and so we go on an evening walk instead.
